Our Mission

An amputation impacts far more than the individual. It affects spouses, children, parents, caregivers, friends, and everyone who walks beside them.

Lost Limb Riders created the Family Support Initiative to provide education, encouragement, fellowship, and resources for the entire family throughout the recovery journey.

Who Can Participate?

  • Spouses and partners
  • Children, parents, grandparents, and siblings
  • Caregivers and immediate family members
  • Trusted support persons walking alongside recovery

Support For The Whole Family

The initiative connects families to orientation, peer encouragement, events, and resource information that help rebuild hope and independence.

Family Orientation

Every new member and family is welcomed through an orientation introducing Lost Limb Riders, volunteer opportunities, event safety, membership expectations, and available support resources.

Support Services

Monthly family support meetings, peer-to-peer encouragement, caregiver groups, educational workshops, cookouts, holiday celebrations, community events, and hospital or rehabilitation visits.

Children Matter Too

Family-friendly activities, games, educational events, and community gatherings give children space to be kids while meeting others who understand limb loss.

Community Resources

  • Rehabilitation services and prosthetic providers
  • Veterans resources and disability benefits information
  • Employment, housing, and transportation resources
  • Counseling referrals and mental health resource information

Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Treat every family with dignity and respect confidentiality.
  • Listen without judgment and avoid medical or legal advice.
  • Encourage hope, independence, and professional representation of Lost Limb Riders.

Get Connected With Family Support

When families receive encouragement, education, and community, everyone grows stronger. Reach out to learn more, volunteer, or help a family connect with Lost Limb Riders.
